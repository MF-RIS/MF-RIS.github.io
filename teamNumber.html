Team Mumber

Hui Tian

Hui Tian (Senior Member, IEEE) received the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), China, in 1992 and 2003, respectively. Currently, she is a professor with the School of Information and Communication Engineering at BUPT. Her current research interests mainly include radio resource management in 5G/6G networks, mobile edge computing, cooperative communication, mobile social network, and Internet of Things.

Wanli Ni

Wanli Ni (Member, IEEE) received the B.Eng. and Ph.D. degrees in the School of Information and Communication Engineering from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), China, in 2018 and 2023, respectively. From September 2020 to June 2022, he was a Visiting Student (remote) with the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science, Queen Mary University of London (QMUL), U.K., under the supervision of Prof. Yuanwei Liu. From July 2022 to September 2022, he was a Visiting Student with the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech), Shenzhen, China, under the supervision of Prof. Changsheng You. From December 2022 to June 2023, he was a Visiting Student with the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, under the supervision of Prof. Dusit Niyato. Currently, he is a Research Assistant in the Department of Electronic Engineering at Tsinghua University, China, under the supervision of Prof. Zhijin Qin. His research interests include federated learning (FL), reconfigurable intelligent surface (RIS), non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA), over-the-air computation (AirComp), integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), machine learning in wireless communications, performance analysis and optimization of wireless networks.
He was a recipient of the Samsung Scholarship in 2019,the National Scholarship in 2021 and 2022, the Excellent Graduate of Beijing in 2023, the BUPT Outstanding Doctoral Dissertation Award in 2023, the BUPT Top-10 Campus Pioneers on Academic in 2023. He was a co-author of the Best Paper Award in the IEEE SAGC 2020. He was a recipient of the IEEE ComSoc Student Travel Grant from multiple international conferences including IEEE GLOBECOM, INFOCOM, and ICC. He was selected as an Exemplary Reviewer of the IEEE Wireless Communications Letters in 2021, the IEEE Communications Letters in 2022, and the IEEE Transactions on Communications in 2022.

Wen Wang

Wen Wang received the B.Eng. degree in communication engineering from Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), Beijing, China, in 2020, where she is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree with the State Key Laboratory of Networking and Switching Technology. From June 2022 to August 2022, she was a Visiting Student with Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech) under the supervision of Prof. Changsheng You. From September 2022, she is a Visiting Student with National University of Singapore (NUS), Singapore, under the supervision of Prof. Rui Zhang. Her current research interests include reconfigurable intelligent surface (RIS), unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), physical-layer security (PLS), and non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA). She was a recipient of the Samsung Scholarship in 2021 and the Huawei Scholarship in 2022.

Ailing Zheng

Ailing Zheng is a master's student in the School of Information and Communication Engineering from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), China. Her research is focused on the wireless resource management in RIS-related communication scenarios.

Zaihao Pan

Zaihao Pan received the B.Eng. in the School of Information and Communication Engineering from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT), China, in 2023 where he is currently pursuing the M.Eng. degree with with the State Key Laboratory of Networking and Switching Technology. His research interests in Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface (MF-RIS) Aided Communication and its channel modeling, as well as its application in Industrial Internet of thing (IIoT) networks.
